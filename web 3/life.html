Early Life and Education

Adrienne Clarkson and her family obtained a chance to flee to canada during the Japeanese invation of the territory. She arrived at Canada with her family in 1941 as a penniless refugee from Hong Kong and later settled in Ottawa. At the time, Hong-Kong was a Japanese-occupied territory. She studied at Lisgar Collegiate Institute and later went to University of Toronto. After getting er master's degree in English literature at the University of Toronto, she began post-graduate work in 1962.
